Vada

A vada is a fried fritter that does double duty as both a snack and a breakfast item.

Ingredients

  • 3/4 cup Urad dal
  • Water enough to soak
  • Ice cold water as required around 3 tablespoon + as needed
  • Curry leaves few
  • 1/8 teaspoon sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon pepper
  • 1/8 teaspoon ginger powder or grated ginger
  • 2 green Chilli deseeded
  • Salt
  • 1/4 cup Onion
  • Asafoetida generous portion
  • Oil to fry

Instructions

  1. Wash the dal well and soak for 4- 5 hours until the dal splits well if pressed
  2. Transfer the soaked dal to a colander and drain the water
  3. Sprinkle cold water little by little and ground fluffy smooth batter
  4. Mixie or grinder always mix well in between grinding. Wipe the urad dal that sticks to the grinder/ mixie
  5. The batter should be fluffy, half white, and smooth
  6. Take a small amount of ground batter and drop it into the bowl/ glass of water
  7. The batter should maintain its shape and float on top. It should not spread out or dissolve
  8. The batter should be non-sticky to your hands
  9. If the batter is the right consistency transfer and mixes well with your hand in a clockwise direction for 30 to 40 seconds. This makes the batter more fluffy and aerated
  10. Rest the batter - for easy shaping
  11. Cover and refrigerate the batter for just half an hour for easy shaping
  12. Add all the additional ingredients except onions and salt and mix well
  13. Add enough oil to a heavy-bottomed pan and heat
  14. If you drop a small piece of the batter it will raise immediately without browning then the oil is hot enough to fry
  15. Once the oil turns hot, adjust the flame and fry the vadai in medium heat
  16. Just before frying add salt and onions to the batter and mix well
  17. Take water in a small bowl
  18. Gently grease both hands with little water
  19. In one hand take a small portion of the batter and roll. Make sure water is not dripping from the hand
  20. Press it on the other hand and make a hole in the center, carefully add it to the hot oil
  21. Alternatively, you can grease parchment paper / plastic sheet with little water and take a small portion of the batter, roll, and press, make a hole, carefully add it to the hot oil
  22. You can also take a small ball-shaped batter in your right-hand fingers. Roll and make a hole in the center using your right-hand thumb finger .carefully Invert your hand over oil and drop the vada by slightly tilting your finger or slide the vada using your thumb finger
  23. After carefully dropping the vadai in oil
  24. Vadai raises
  25. Fry Vadai on both sides until they turn golden brown
  26. Carefully flip and cook till ulundu vadai turns golden brown on both sides. Serve hot with coconut chutney, sambar
